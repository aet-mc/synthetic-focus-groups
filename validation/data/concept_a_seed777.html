Focus Group Report: A small, screenless wearable device that clips to your clothing and acts as your personal AI assistant. It replaces your smartphone — no screen, fully voice-controlled, always listening for your commands. It can make calls, send messages, answer questions, give directions, and take photos using a tiny projector for visual output. The idea: free yourself from screen addiction while staying connected.

Key Themes

Voice Assistant Functionality Negative

8 of 8 participants

This theme encompasses the core capabilities and performance of voice assistants, including accuracy, reliability, and contextual understanding.

"Certainly, while the convenience of hands-free operation is undeniable, the fallibility of voice recognition introduces a margin for error that could impede seamless functionality. Moreover, contextual nuances and variations in pronunciation can further complicate accurate command interpretation, thereby potentially diminishing the overall user experience."
- Mia
"The crucial aspect for me would be the accuracy and dependability of the voice interface, especially in noisy environments. If it frequently misunderstands commands or requires multiple attempts, the convenience factor diminishes rapidly. The device's ability to accurately interpret and execute instructions is vital for its practicality in my daily life."
- Mia
"To echo Daniel's sentiment, the practicality of projected images as a primary output method seems dubious, especially considering ambient light conditions. While voice commands offer a degree of convenience, their efficacy in lieu of a tangible display for intricate tasks remains questionable, thereby tempering my enthusiasm for this design choice."
- Mia

Mobile Device Integration & Usage Negative

7 of 8 participants

This theme focuses on how the technology interacts with mobile devices, including phone use, navigation, and potential replacements for smartphones.

"The projection aspect seems like a gimmick, and I find it hard to believe it would work well in practice. While voice commands are useful in certain contexts, completely forgoing a screen seems rather limiting for a device that is supposed to replace a smartphone. I suspect the utility would be significantly diminished."
- Daniel
"Certainly, the inconvenience of perpetually seeking a power source for electronic devices is indeed a notable drawback. It constrains mobility and engenders a sense of dependency on infrastructure, which can be particularly vexing when traveling or in situations where outlets are scarce."
- Mia

- Daniel

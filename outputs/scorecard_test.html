Executive Summary

NO-GO

The coffee subscription concept received lukewarm interest, primarily due to concerns about price and perceived value. Participants questioned whether the quality justified the $16 monthly cost, especially compared to readily available store-bought options. The subscription model itself was a barrier for some, who expressed a preference for one-time purchases over recurring commitments. To improve appeal, focus on demonstrating the value proposition through superior quality and flexible subscription terms.

Warmup

Moderator: Alright everyone, to get us started and comfortable, let's go around the table. Tell me, what's your go-to coffee, whether you make it at home or grab it out? David, how about we start with you?

David: I usually just have a regular black coffee. I'm not too particular about brands, and I typically make it at home to save money. It's nothing fancy.

Amelia: Well, David, I commend your simplicity. I tend to favor a cappuccino in the mornings, either crafted at home with my modest espresso machine or procured from a local café if I'm on the go. While I appreciate the convenience of brewing at home, sometimes the ambiance of a coffee shop is an invigorating change of pace.

Mia: I typically prepare a pour-over at home, using a medium-roast Ethiopian bean. It offers a consistent flavor profile that I appreciate, and preparing it myself ensures quality control.

Anthony: I usually just have drip coffee. Whichever brand is on sale at the grocery store. Nothing special.

Exploration

Moderator: Okay, thanks for sharing everyone. David and Amelia, we haven't heard from you in a little while. Thinking about coffee in general, what's the first thing that comes to mind when you hear the words "specialty coffee"?

David: Overpriced coffee comes to mind. I'm sure there are subtle differences, but I doubt they justify the price hike. I'd need some convincing.

Amelia: Intriguing question. I immediately think of meticulously sourced beans and artisanal roasting techniques, which, while often justifying a higher price point, can sometimes be a tad pretentious. It really all hinges on whether the final cup delivers on its promise of superior flavor and aroma.

Anthony: Too expensive. I don't trust that "specialty" means it's worth the extra cost. Usually it's just marketing.

Deep Dive

Moderator: David, you mentioned overpriced coffee. Can you elaborate on what makes a coffee feel overpriced to you?

David: For me, it's about the diminishing returns. A slight upgrade in taste isn't worth double or triple the price. I'd rather save that money or spend it elsewhere.

Mia: I tend to agree with David’s assessment regarding diminishing returns. While I appreciate a well-sourced bean, I find that some cafes leverage the term "specialty" to justify prices that far exceed the actual value or tangible difference in quality. It's crucial to strike a balance between quality and cost.

Amelia: I concur with David that the price needs to correspond to a tangible improvement in the beverage. While I am willing to pay a premium for superior quality, there's a limit to what I deem justifiable, especially when considering my fixed income as a retiree. It all boils down to whether the enhanced flavor profile warrants the additional cost.

Reaction

Moderator: Okay, we've heard some initial thoughts on the concept. Anthony and Mia, I'd love to hear from you. What are your initial reactions to a coffee subscription like this one? Does it pique your interest at all?

Anthony: Not really. I prefer to buy my coffee at the store. Subscriptions are risky, and I don't want to get stuck with something I don't like.

Mia: I share Anthony’s risk aversion regarding subscriptions. The proposition would need to offer a very flexible cancellation policy. Otherwise, I would worry about being stuck with coffee that doesn't meet my preferences.

David: A coffee subscription could be interesting, depending on the price. Free shipping is a plus, but I'd want to be able to pause or cancel easily if needed. I'm open to trying it, but the value has to be there.

Amelia: Well, while I appreciate the concerns regarding commitment, the ability to skip at any time assuages some of those fears. For a mere $16 a month, the proposition of freshly roasted specialty beans delivered gratis seems rather tempting, provided the quality matches the allure. I find it a palatable starting point to explore novel flavors without undue financial strain.

Synthesis

Moderator: Okay, we've heard some interesting perspectives. Let's try to synthesize everything. In your own words, what's the *single* biggest reason that would either make you sign up for this coffee subscription or prevent you from doing so? Anthony, I want your take as well.

Anthony: I wouldn't sign up. I don't like subscriptions. I'd rather buy coffee at the store so I know what I'm getting.

David: The price point is the single biggest factor. If $16 delivers decent coffee reliably, I'm in. But if the quality is lacking, I'll cancel immediately.

Mia: The most salient factor influencing my decision is the consistency of coffee quality. While the price is reasonable, I would need assurances regarding the quality control measures to mitigate the risk of receiving subpar products.
